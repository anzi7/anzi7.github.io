Research

Research Statement

My research focuses on the Inverse Eigenvalue Problem for Graphs (IEPG). I investigate which spectra can be realized by real symmetric matrices whose zero-nonzero patterns are constrained by an underlying graph structure — exploring spectral properties, multiplicities, and the interplay between combinatorial structures and algebraic invariants of graphs.

A major goal of my work is to provide constructive methods to realize prescribed spectra. I combine combinatorial techniques with algebraic and matrix-theoretic methods. All constructions and validations are carefully verified using Python and SageMath; code and notebooks are available on my GitHub. I am also interested in extremal spectral graph theory, matrix inertia, eigenvalue multiplicity obstructions, and algorithmic approaches for spectral invariants, with applications to network analysis and combinatorial optimization.
